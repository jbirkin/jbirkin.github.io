3-dimensional spectral fitting

In the modern world of galaxy evolution, spatially resolved observations of high-redshift galaxies are becoming increasingly more possible, and more necessary to help us understand their properties. My research has involved extensive use of integral field units, which provide 3-D data cubes covering the target: essentially a 2-D image with 1-D spectra taken at each pixel. To analyze such data I wrote a tool to model emission lines in IFU data on a pixel-by-pixel basis, which can be found here. This is very much a work in progress and I welcome any suggestions on how to improve it.
