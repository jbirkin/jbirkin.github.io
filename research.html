Data-driven astronomy projects

3-D modelling of JWST data

I am a member of the international collaboration TEMPLATES, an on-going programme designed to spatially resolve the star formation in four galaxies at the peak of cosmic star formation. These galaxies are gravitationally lensed, meaning that they appear much brighter than they do intrinsically, and they can be observed at much higher spatial resolutions. Overall, this allows an incredibly detailed study of the galaxies. As an expert in near-infrared IFU obeservations, my role is to extract the relevant data features to give a complete picture of the underlying systems while effectively visualizing the data. To this end, I developed a powerful open-source Python module which simultaneously models the emission from the galaxy and produces a complete visual of the entire cube. I utilized this tool to produce this peer-reviewed publication in the Astrophysical Journal, which has been cited over 20 times.

Left: Comparing measurements of system properties with existing scaling relations. Right: Identifying trends between galaxy dust mass and galaxy dust enrichment.

Aggregating and stacking of spectral data for model selection

As a member of the international CRISTAL: [CII] Resolved ISM in Star-Forming Galaxies with ALMA collaboration, my role is to combine the 3-D data from CRISTAL to boost the signal-to-noise ratio and identify faint emission features. For this project I have utilized dimensionality reduction techniques, data normalization, MCMC model fitting, model selection, bootstrap/jacknife resampling and feature engineering.

Left: Aggregated and combined spectral data for 15 sources. The data are well-modelled by two Gaussian components. Right: 2-D emission maps extracted from the 3-D data cubes.

Data-Driven Insights into Turbulent Galactic Disks

In grad school I led the processing and analysis of a ~300GB Large Program, with the goal of understanding the 2-D kinematics of highly star-forming systems. The data processing and cleaning involved PCA analysis and sigma clipping to reduce noise. After cross-matching the data with existing databases, I applied an adaptive binning technique to model the 3-D data and extract 1-D rotation curves for the galaxies. Below is a visualization of the rotation fields of each galaxy in the sample, plotted as a function of two basic system properties. This work was published in the Monthly Notices of the Royal Astronomical Society, and has been cited over a dozen times.

Molecular gas in dusty star-forming galaxies

Leveraging data from state-of-the-art facilities, I modelled the radio emission of early-Universe galaxies to provide insights into their underlying properties. To prepare this dataset I utilized advanced algorithmic cleaning and feature extraction from multi-baseline observations, which I modelled using Bayesian Inference. I combined my measurements with a wide variety of archival data using catalog matching techniques, which enhanced the robustness of the statistical conclusions I was able to draw. This work was published in the Monthly Notices of the Royal Astronomical Society, and has been cited over 80 times.
